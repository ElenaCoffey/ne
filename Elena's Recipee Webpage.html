Recipe #1 - Pelmeni

  • Time: 2 hours (don't worry, it will last for a week)
  • Serves: 4 people for a week
Ingredients Quantity
Flour 4 cups
eggs 2
water 1 cup
ground beaf 1 cup
ground pork 1 cup
onion 2 big head

Step 1: Make a dow by mixing eggs, water, and flour. Add some salt. Mix it adding flour to it untill it has a consistency of a playdow. Wrap it into food plastic or put it into plastic bag, sill and let it stand for 30 minutes.

Step 2: In a meanwhile, chop the onion into smal cubes(1/8 of inch), mix beaf, pork, chopped onion, salt, and a 1/4cup of water together. Let it stand in refrigerator to releave some juices for about 15 min.

Step 3: Make dumplings. Put them into boiling water with half head of onion, the rest must be freezed in ref. Cook in boiling water until they start to float. When they are - they are ready. Take them out or eat them like a soup.

Recipe #2 - Grechnevaya kasha

  • Time: 30 minutes
  • Serves: 4 people
Ingredients Quantity
Buckweat 1 cups
water 3 cups
salt tip of a teaspoon
butter 4 table spoons

Step 1: Boil buckweat in pan, then simmer till water completely evaporates. Don't mess the point when it does completely, so your kasha do not start to burn.

Step 2: Pour it into bowl, add salt or honey if necessary for your taste.

Step 3: Add 1 table spoon of butter and milk if you wish. Enjoy!

Recipe #3 - Herring under Cover

  • Time: 2 hours (don't worry, it will last for a week)
  • Serves: 4 people for a couple of days
Ingredients Quantity
Herring fillet 1 medium fish
eggs 2
beets 4 heads
onion 1 big head
carrots 2
mayonese 6 table spoons

Step 1: Cook beets, carrots, potatoes, eggs and onions. Clean and cut into small pieces the herring fillet.

Step 2: Chop all cooked vegetables and eggs into small peaces. Put them in the layers in such order in a bowl; potatos, onions, herring,carrots, beets, and eggs. Inbetween, put a thin layer of mayo. Distiribute it evenly.

Step 3: Decorate the topping with some greens and carrots peaces. Let settle in refrigarator for about 2 hours. And it's ready! Have a good appetite! as we, Russians, say before the meal to our guests. Enjoy!
